Do NOT file quantum beat signals that frame governance debates, community disagreements, or BIP interpretation angles — even with notable quotes from core devs or cryptographers.

**Why:** Three consecutive quantum signals rejected (April 17-18, 2026): "BIP-361 Updated: Bitcoin Proposes Freezing 6.9M Quantum-Vulnerable BTC", "Bitcoin Quantum Governance Fractures — Adam Back Rejects Forced Freeze", "Bitcoin Devs Float 'Quantum Tripwire' — Conditional Freeze Triggers Only If Attack Is Proved On-Chain". All were angles on the same ongoing governance discussion. Governance debate framing fails the newsworthy gate — it's a condition persisting, not an event. The quantum beat is saturated with this type of signal.

**How to apply:** On the quantum beat, only file on: (1) specific hardware milestones (new qubit count records, error rate thresholds crossed), (2) formally submitted BIPs or research reaching a new stage (merged into BIP repo, NIST standard selected), or (3) published arXiv/peer-reviewed papers with specific claims about Bitcoin cryptography timelines. Developer tweets and community debates about quantum policy are not quantum beat news regardless of how novel the framing.
